# AutoSales AI - Sales Playbook

> This document defines the sales process, email best practices, follow-up cadence,
> qualification criteria, and rules that all AutoSales AI agents follow.

---

## SALES PROCESS STAGES

The pipeline follows these stages, matching the CRM (Pipedrive) pipeline:

| Stage | Agent | Description | Exit Criteria |
|-------|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **1. New Lead** | - | Raw lead enters the system (import, scrape, manual) | Has email + company name |
| **2. Research** | Agent #5 | Company and person research, scoring | Research report complete, score assigned |
| **3. Qualified** | Qualifier | Lead scored 40+, initial outreach prepared | First email draft approved/sent |
| **4. First Contact** | Qualifier | First outreach email sent | Email delivered successfully |
| **5. Follow-Up** | Follow-Up | No response; follow-up sequence active | Response received OR 3 attempts exhausted |
| **6. Engaged** | - (human) | Prospect responded; conversation active | Meeting booked or quote requested |
| **7. Proposal** | - (human) | Quote or samples sent | Decision pending |
| **8. Negotiation** | - (human) | Terms being discussed | Agreement reached or lost |
| **9. Won** | - | Deal closed | Order placed |
| **10. Lost** | - | Deal lost or prospect disqualified | Reason logged |
| **11. Nurture** | Follow-Up | Not ready now, revisit later | Scheduled for future outreach |

## QUALIFICATION CRITERIA

### Auto-Qualify (Score 55+)
Leads with a research score of 55 or above are automatically queued for first outreach.

### Manual Review Required (Score 40-54)
Leads in this range need human review before outreach begins.

### Auto-Disqualify (Score < 25)
- Competitors (other chocolate manufacturers)
- Consumers (not B2B)
- Completely unrelated industries
- Invalid or bounced email addresses

### Qualification Signals (Positive)
- Company is in food/confectionery/retail/distribution
- Contact is in purchasing, procurement, buying, or management role
- Company operates in ADIKAM's target markets
- Evidence of importing or distributing chocolate products
- Seasonal buying patterns visible

### Disqualification Signals (Negative)
- Company is a direct competitor
- Email domain is generic consumer (gmail, yahoo for company email)
- No connection to food industry whatsoever
- Company appears to be dormant or out of business

## EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

### Subject Lines
- Keep under 50 characters when possible
- Mention a specific benefit or product category
- Personalize with company name or market reference
- Avoid spam triggers (ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, "free", "buy now")
- Examples:
  - "Private label chocolate for [Company]?"
  - "Easter 2027 chocolate range - partnership idea"
  - "Premium chocolate supply for [Market] market"

### Email Body Structure
1. **Opening** (1-2 sentences): Reference something specific about their business
2. **Value Proposition** (2-3 sentences): Why ADIKAM is relevant to them
3. **Specific Offer** (1-2 sentences): Concrete next step (samples, catalog, call)
4. **Call to Action** (1 sentence): Single, clear ask
5. **Signature**: Professional sign-off with full contact details

### Rules
- Maximum 4 short paragraphs
- Maximum 150 words for first outreach
- Always include a specific product or seasonal hook
- One clear call-to-action per email
- Professional signature with name, title, company, website
- No attachments in first email (offer to send catalog if interested)

## FOLLOW-UP CADENCE

### Standard Follow-Up Sequence

| Attempt | Timing | Type | Strategy |
|---------|--------|------|----------|
| **1st Follow-Up** | Day +5 | Gentle Reminder | Short, reference original email, add new angle |
| **2nd Follow-Up** | Day +14 | Value Add | Share relevant seasonal info, industry insight, or case study |
| **3rd Follow-Up** | Day +30 | Final Attempt | Direct close-or-archive question; offer to reconnect later |

### Follow-Up Rules
- Never follow up more than 3 times without a response
- If prospect replies asking to stop, immediately cease contact and mark as opted out
- If prospect replies with interest, escalate to human sales team
- If prospect replies with "not now", move to Nurture stage with a reminder date
- Minimum 5 calendar days between any two emails to the same person
- Do not follow up on weekends or local holidays

### After 3rd Follow-Up (No Response)
- Move lead to "Nurture" stage
- Schedule re-engagement in 3-6 months
- Re-engagement should reference a new seasonal opportunity or product launch

## ESCALATION RULES

### Escalate to Human Sales Team When:
1. Prospect responds with specific product inquiries or pricing requests
2. Prospect wants to schedule a call or meeting
3. Prospect requests samples
4. Order value appears to exceed 10 pallets
5. Prospect is a major retail chain or enterprise account
6. Prospect raises concerns, complaints, or complex questions
7. Legal or contractual questions arise
8. Prospect requests custom certifications or audits

### How to Escalate
- Update CRM with full conversation history
- Create a task/notification for the assigned sales rep
- Draft a handoff summary including:
  - Research report highlights
  - Communication history
  - Prospect's specific interests or questions
  - Recommended next steps

## DO'S AND DON'TS

### DO
- Research every prospect before outreach
- Personalize every single email
- Reference specific products relevant to their business
- Respect timezone and business hours
- Track all interactions in the CRM
- Learn from response patterns to improve future outreach
- Use the prospect's language when possible
- Follow up with purpose (new information, seasonal angle)
- Keep a professional, partnership-oriented tone
- Include opt-out option in all emails

### DON'T
- Send generic template emails
- Contact the same person from multiple addresses
- Promise specific prices without current price list verification
- Fabricate product specifications or certifications
- Use aggressive, pushy, or manipulative language
- Send attachments in first contact (offer to send upon interest)
- Follow up more than 3 times without a response
- Email outside business hours when timezone is known
- Share one prospect's information with another
- Make claims about competitors
- Use informal language or slang in B2B outreach
- Send bulk emails without personalization
